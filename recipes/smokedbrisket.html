Smoked Brisket

A delicious slow cooked beef dinner, best served with family and friends

This meal takes a little time to prepare, but a lot of time to cook. Required: patience and a smoker

Ingredients

  • Brisket (point or flat)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic

Additional items needed

  • Butcher paper(Aluminum pan with foil works)
  • wood chunks (Post oak works best)

Steps

  1. Prepare smoker for indirect heat: 250F(121C).
  2. trim excess fat from brisket, down to 1/4"(.63cm)
  3. season all sides with salt, pepper, and garlic.
  4. Place brisket on smoker, add wood chunks
  5. smoke for 5 hours, maintaining consistent heat and smoke
  6. check internal temperature, and continue to smoke until reaches 160F(71C)
  7. Once reaches temperature, wrap brisket in two layers of butcher paper and return to smoker
    • if you only have aluminum pan and foil, place brisket in pan and wrap and return to smoker
  8. continue cooking until internal temperature reaches 200F(93.3C)
  9. remove brisket and let rest for 2 hours
    • a dry cooler is preferred, but can rest on counter
  10. If you have point brisket, separate sections. Slice flat against grain to your preferred width, Cut point in half, and slice against grain. Edges are burnt ends if cubed.
  11. enjoy!
